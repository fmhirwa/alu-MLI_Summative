What is African Leadership University (ALU)?  
ALU is committed to transforming higher education in Africa by developing ethical and entrepreneurial leaders. It has campuses in Rwanda and Mauritius and offers a unique learning experience focused on real-world problem-solving and leadership development.












Where are ALU campuses located?  
ALU has campuses in Rwanda and Mauritius.












What makes ALU's learning experience unique?  
ALU emphasizes real-world problem-solving, leadership development, and encourages students to declare missions rather than traditional majors.












What is the BSc (Hons) International Business and Trade program about?  
It prepares leaders to navigate global business complexities and drive Africa’s economic integration, covering topics like international trade, regional integration, and global supply chain management.












What is the duration of the BSc (Hons) International Business and Trade program?  
3 years.












What is the BSc (Hons) Entrepreneurial Leadership program about?  
It nurtures future leaders with entrepreneurial skills, focusing on innovation, problem-solving, and leadership through experiential learning, including internships and community projects.












What is the duration of the BSc (Hons) Entrepreneurial Leadership program?  
3 years.












What is the BSc (Hons) Software Engineering program about?  
Offered in partnership with Holberton School, it equips students with software engineering skills, including computer programming, software design, application development, and system architecture.












What is the duration of the BSc (Hons) Software Engineering program?  
3 years.












Are ALU’s degrees accredited?  
Yes, all degrees at ALU Rwanda are accredited by the Higher Education Council (HEC) of Rwanda.












What are the general admission requirements at ALU?  
Applicants must have at least two principal passes in final national secondary school examinations, demonstrate English proficiency at a B2 level, and be at least 16 years old.












How do I apply to ALU?  
The application process involves completing a basic form, submitting supporting documents, and understanding financial commitments.












Are there specific requirements for ALU programs?  
Yes. IBT requires interest in international trade; Entrepreneurial Leadership requires leadership potential; Software Engineering requires a strong foundation in mathematics.












How much is the tuition fee at ALU?  
The annual tuition fee is $3,000.












Does ALU offer financial aid?  
Yes, ALU offers need-based scholarships and grants, prioritizing females, refugees, displaced individuals, and those with disabilities.












Are there flexible payment plans available at ALU?  
Yes, flexible payment plans are available. Details can be obtained from the admissions office.












Who are ALU and ALC?  
ALU (Kigali, Rwanda) and ALC (Mauritius) are sister institutions offering undergraduate programs with access to networking hubs in various cities.












Why do Rwanda and Mauritius campuses offer different degrees?  
To meet specific national accreditation requirements and align with educational standards in each country.












Is the Mauritius campus still open?  
Yes, ALC Mauritius is open, with continuing students pursuing programs, and plans to launch new programs.












Do applicants choose a campus after acceptance?  
No, applicants must select their intended campus during the application process.












What is the difference between ALU, ALC, SOWC, and ALUSB?  
These are distinct schools within the ALU network, each offering unique undergraduate or graduate programs with opportunities for collaboration.












Am I eligible to apply to ALU?  
ALU welcomes applicants who have completed secondary education and are committed to shaping Africa’s future.












What is the admissions process at ALU?  
The process involves submitting academic documents, understanding financial commitments, and applying for financial aid if needed.












Is the ALU application only available online?  
Yes, the application process is exclusively online.












Are there age requirements for applying to ALU?  
Applicants of all ages are welcome, but those under 18 need parental consent.












Can I transfer to ALU from another university?  
Yes, but transfer credits are reviewed individually and are not guaranteed.












How long does it take to receive an admissions decision?  
Decisions are typically provided within two weeks of submission.












What documents are required for the ALU application?  
National exam transcripts, secondary school transcripts (Grades 10-12), and an identification document.












What are the different admission decisions at ALU?  
Decisions include Offer, Conditional Offer, Preliminary Offer, Waitlisted, and Rejected.












Are there additional costs beyond tuition?  
Yes, students should budget for accommodation, meals, study materials, and personal expenses.












What types of financial aid does ALU offer?  
ALU provides need-based grants and scholarships that apply to tuition fees and do not require repayment.
